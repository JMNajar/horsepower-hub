01

Data Readiness as a Competitive Advantage

Build the foundation for AI with trusted, accessible, well-governed data.

The Reality: Most AI Failures Are Data Failures

When AI initiatives stall, leaders often blame the tool. In reality, the breakdown usually occurs much earlier — in the data itself. Before investing in new AI capabilities, organizations must answer three questions:

  • Where does our critical data live?
  • Who owns it?
  • Can we trust it?

If those questions are difficult to answer, scaling AI will amplify risk rather than productivity.

Common Barriers to AI Scale

  • Fragmented systems
  • Outdated or incomplete data
  • Siloed ownership
  • Lack of governance
  • Inconsistent definitions across teams

What "AI-Ready Data" Means for an SMB

AI-readiness does not mean enterprise-scale transformation overnight. If monthly reports require manual reconciliation, or finance and operations use different definitions of the same metric — AI scale will be constrained. AI-ready means:

  • Core operational data is mapped
  • Every critical dataset has a clear owner
  • Reporting logic is documented
  • Manual reconciliation is minimized
  • Access and permissions are defined

⚠ Warning Signs You Need to Prioritize Data Readiness

  • Reports require manual intervention
  • Departments define the same metrics differently
  • AI pilot outputs are inconsistent
  • No one is accountable for data quality
  • System integration projects repeatedly stall
30-60-90 Day Action Plan
Days 1–30 · Establish Visibility
  • Map core systems and workflows
  • Document where critical data lives
  • Assign accountable owners for datasets
  • Connect with TechPoint CDO Network
Days 31–60 · Stabilize One Workflow
  • Select one friction-creating workflow
  • Standardize definitions
  • Reduce manual reconciliation
  • Clarify access controls
Days 61–90 · Integrate AI
  • Define measurable KPIs
  • Track performance changes
  • Document learnings for replication
  • Explore AI use case repository
02

Building the Workforce AI Can't Replace

AI adoption depends equally on people and technology. Prepare through change management and practical upskilling — not replacement.

Augmentation Over Replacement

AI excels at codified work — tasks that are routine, rules-based, and well documented. Humans excel at judgment, context, communication, and accountability. Organizations that struggle with AI adoption often deploy tools without preparing their people. Tools pile onto existing workloads, managers lack capacity, and adoption stalls.

What AI-Ready Talent Actually Requires

Organizations don't need machine learning engineers everywhere. Teams need operational AI fluency:

  • Effective prompt structuring
  • AI output validation
  • Sensitive data protection awareness
  • Understanding when human oversight is necessary
  • Workflow adaptation for automation

Underutilized Strategy: Early-Career Talent

Deploy interns and early-career professionals on foundational AI work — they gain real experience while strengthening your organization's infrastructure:

  • Data cleanup
  • Process documentation
  • Workflow mapping
  • AI tool testing
  • Knowledge capture

⚠ Warning Signs

  • AI tools deployed without structured training
  • Managers unclear on measuring AI-enabled productivity
  • Employees experimenting with tools outside governance frameworks
  • Rising workforce anxiety
  • Reactive rather than planned upskilling conversations
30-60-90 Day Action Plan
Days 1–30 · Assess & Align
  • Message: "AI = productivity tool, not replacement"
  • Identify roles exposed to AI-enabled change
  • Break roles into task-level components
  • Determine routine vs. judgment-based tasks
Days 31–60 · Build Capacity
  • Launch pilot AI literacy cohort
  • Train on prompting, governance, workflow redesign
  • Deploy early-career support on foundational projects
  • Managers: document execution vs. innovation conflicts
Days 61–90 · Measure & Codify
  • Track productivity improvements
  • Refine role expectations
  • Formalize augmentation strategy
  • Engage Indiana AI Innovation Network
03

Smart Innovation Strategy: Build, Buy, or Partner?

Identify appropriate opportunities, align investments to business goals, and make disciplined decisions — before spending.

Discipline Over Hype

Most small and mid-sized businesses don't require custom AI systems. They benefit from disciplined integration of existing tools that make sense for their environment. The most successful organizations focus on integrating mature platforms — not developing proprietary models.

"Buying another tool will not fix foundational gaps."

5 Questions Before Any AI Investment

  • Does this solve a clearly-defined business problem?
  • Is our data stable enough to support it?
  • Will this integrate with existing systems?
  • Is ROI measurable and time-bound?
  • Who owns implementation and accountability?

Common SMB Mistakes

  • Expanding pilots without governance structures
  • Overestimating automation capabilities
  • Underinvesting in change management
  • Failing to discontinue underperforming tools
30-60-90 Day Action Plan
Days 1–30 · Define the Use Case
  • Identify one high-impact, measurable use case
  • Establish baseline performance metrics
  • Map system dependencies
Days 31–60 · Pilot with Guardrails
  • Deploy tightly scoped pilot
  • Train small, accountable user group
  • Track KPIs weekly
  • Do not expand scope mid-pilot
Days 61–90 · Decide with Discipline
  • Scale only if ROI is measurable
  • Revise if partial progress exists
  • Sunset if performance doesn't justify cost
04

Infrastructure & Sustainability

AI requires appropriate technical and operational conditions. Address infrastructure, compute costs, and vendor risk before scaling.

AI Is Not Virtual

Despite appearing intangible, every AI workflow operates on physical infrastructure — data centers, compute power, energy grids, and water-cooled systems. As adoption scales, these physical requirements shape long-term cost structures. Small businesses need to account for:

  • Cloud pricing volatility
  • Increased compute consumption
  • Vendor dependency risk
  • Regulatory shifts

⚠ Warning Signs for Reassessment

  • Compute costs are not visible in ROI models
  • Vendor contracts lack transparency
  • AI growth projections assume flat cloud costs
  • Sustainability commitments are disconnected from AI expansion
30-60-90 Day Action Plan
Days 1–30 · Cost Visibility
  • Audit AI-related cloud usage
  • Review vendor contracts
  • Document compute-related expenses
Days 31–60 · Pilot with Guardrails
  • Incorporate compute costs into AI ROI modeling
  • Assess vendor diversification options
  • Align AI expansion with financial planning
Days 61–90 · Formalize Governance
  • Establish oversight for AI infrastructure decisions
  • Document cost and risk monitoring cadence
  • Align AI growth projections with operating strategy
  • Engage ecosystem peers on sustainability
