The Thesis

Horsepower AI is building a focused, repeatable sales engine to resell LaunchReady's Get Found — a managed AI-search visibility service — into Indianapolis-area service businesses, starting with roofing companies. The site at horsepowerai.ai has one job: convert a skeptical, pitch-fatigued local business owner into a booked call, using Jeff's own AI-visibility proof as the credibility hook rather than generic claims.

The model is sequential by design. One vertical is proven first — real prospects, a real case study, a real founding client — before expanding to cosmetic dentistry and personal injury law. Geography follows the same logic: Indianapolis and its suburbs first, then Indiana's larger cities, then the rest of the state, then North Carolina.

Three terms anchor the positioning: Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) as the technical category, AI Search Visibility as the plain-English customer-facing version, and Get Found as the branded product itself — each paired with "Indianapolis" to keep the claim winnable rather than competing nationally.

The engine has four moving parts: a lead-capture site that owns attribution instead of redirecting traffic straight to LaunchReady, AEO-optimized content that answers real buyer questions, a newsletter that nurtures prospects who aren't ready yet, and a prospect tracker that turns outreach into a visible, working pipeline. This is a deliberately narrow bet: prove the model works once, with real revenue and a real client story, before scaling it wider.

Section 01

Website Build Spec Live — v3

The horsepowerai.ai site, spec'd end to end: design system, funnel architecture, and the "Gemini Visibility Check" — a real, live AI-lookup assessment that scores a roofing company's actual answer-engine presence, not a self-reported guess.

  • Core mechanic: visitor enters business name and service area, a real Gemini API query set runs live, and the result is a genuine measured score — not an estimate dressed up as one.
  • Attribution-safe by design: every lead is captured on Horsepower's own infrastructure before any handoff to LaunchReady — no CTA ever redirects raw traffic off-domain.
  • Scoring methodology: a versioned 5-query set, exact point-by-point formulas, and business-matching rules that prevent false-positive scores from generic business names.
  • Corrected under review: three rounds of external critique caught real mistakes along the way — a homeowner-vs-buyer SEO targeting error and a fabricated "free" API cost claim — both fixed rather than left in place.
