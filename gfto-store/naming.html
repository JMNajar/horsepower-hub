Storefront Naming

How the masthead brand was chosen: three rounds, eight lanes, one seal, and the full candidate table.

← Back to the Working Brief

The Strategic Ground Rules

1. The formulator's name stays off the masthead. "Rhodes"-based names were eliminated: an established pharmaceutical company already operates under the Rhodes name (trademark collision risk in adjacent classes), and naming the whole store after one person creates key-man risk. Instead, "Formulated by Mark Rhodes" becomes a seal — a clickable trust badge on every product page, linking to the About Mark story. The credibility survives; the risk doesn't.

2. The technology stays one click deep. DeeperGreen is a proof point, not a storefront name. The store owns the explanation of the technology on its own Science page so a curious customer never has to search for it.

3. Lifestyle-first, not clinic-first. The first warm audiences — motorcycle lifestyle, cannabis-adjacent consumers, pet rescue communities — shop by feel and identity, not by lab coat. Early medical/clinical name lanes were deprioritized for this reason.

The Shortlist, Ranked

  • 1. Open Road Supply Co. — best culture-fit to the actual first thousand customers; compatible with motorcycle-lifestyle partnerships if those revive.
  • 2. Even Keel (Supply) — the only name that carries the science claim emotionally; the strongest pure-brand idea on the board and the one to test head-to-head against Open Road.
  • 3. Bench & Bottle — the bridge pick if leadership wants craft warmth and visible lab DNA.
  • 4. Placer Supply Co. — the most ownable story, if teaching pronunciation is acceptable.
  • 5. Foothill Supply Co. — the safe, solid regional pick.

Whichever name wins, two assets ride along unchanged: the "Formulated by Mark Rhodes" seal on every product page, and the consumer-facing Science page that owns the delivery-technology story.

Next step before committing: live domain + social-handle availability checks and a USPTO knockout search on the top three.
