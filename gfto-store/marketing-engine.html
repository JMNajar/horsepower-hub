Deep Dive · Category 07

The Marketing Engine

Four subsystems, one weekly loop: AI does the volume, data picks the winners, a human guards the claims — about one hour of human time a week.

← Back to the Working Brief

The Loop

Marketing here isn't a list of tactics — it's one loop that runs every week, forever, and gets smarter each cycle because last week's results are an input to next week's content.

Subsystem 1 — Audience: Who Gets What

  • The home-page quiz tags every subscriber by the state they're chasing — sharper, calmer, better-rested, or "for my dog" — at the moment of capture.
  • Enrichment (OuterSignal → Klaviyo) turns buyers into real profiles, so segments are built from who's actually buying, not guesses.
  • Purchase history moves people through lifecycle stages automatically: prospect → first-time buyer → subscriber → cross-sector customer.
  • The messaging lane (SMS-clean vs. push-only) is a property on every product — compliance is structural, not remembered.

AI's job: maintain all of it. The human's job: nothing after setup.

Subsystem 2 — Content: The Efficiency Multiplier

The core move: one hero piece per sector per week, cascaded into ~12 assets. AI drafts one strong piece — a customer story, a "why you crash at 2pm" explainer, a ritual demo — then derives everything from it: the sector email, 3–4 social posts, the push notification, a YouTube script, the blog post, a review-request variant, a landing-page headline test. One approved idea = twelve approved assets.

  • Brand voice files (a half-page per brand) keep the house of brands from collapsing into one AI-flavored voice.
  • The claims guardrail screens every draft against allowed structure/function language before a human ever sees it — for this catalog, the difference between an automation asset and a liability generator.
  • Reviews are fuel: photo reviews get woven into the next week's content — customers write the best copy.

Subsystem 3 — Distribution: Owned First, Paid Last

Every channel play, with AI's role attached, lives in the Marketing Channel Map on the Tech Stack page — email and push as the engine room, SMS on the clean lane, Facebook/Instagram organic plus community participation, YouTube and SEO as the compounding assets, affiliates and influencer seeding, podcast guesting for the formulator, and paid ads as the last rung.

The strategic spine: for this product category, owned and earned channels aren't the warm-up — they're the engine. Ad platforms will always restrict half the catalog, so paid traffic is a multiplier added to something already converting, never the foundation. The store's AI Search Visibility (AEO) checklist extends the same logic to the newest discovery surface: being the source AI engines quote.

Subsystem 4 — Learning: What Makes It Effective, Not Just Efficient

  • Sunday night: the dashboard pulls everything; the scoreboard is computed.
  • Monday 6am: the summary lands — revenue, repeat rate, best cross-sell path per sector, one decision flagged when one is needed.
  • The closed loop: last week's numbers shape this week's content batch automatically — "morning-themed sleep subject lines doubled click-through" becomes next Monday's brief without a meeting.
  • Monthly: the winning cross-sell path gets promoted from email into on-site placement and bundles.
  • Quarterly: AI drafts challenger variants for the two weakest flow emails; A/B; keep winners.

Total human time: ~1 hour per week to run marketing for the full brand portfolio. The human never writes — they edit. And the 30 minutes of editing is the one step that should never be automated away for a supplements catalog.

The Pre-Launch Play

The engine starts 4 weeks before the store opens. The quiz, the sector lead magnets, the content cascade, and the affiliate kit all work without a checkout — so opening day arrives with a segmented list, warmed-up social accounts, and content muscle memory instead of a cold start. The engine doesn't need a store; it needs an audience. That's also the cheapest customer acquisition the store will ever get.
