Why Shopify wins — honestly: it does not win on sticker price (BigCommerce matches it), and it carries one real flag: if kava fails Shopify Payments' acceptable-use review, a third-party high-risk gateway adds a 2% penalty on the Basic plan. Quantified: at $10K/month of kava revenue that's $200/month — annoying, not architecture-changing, and it applies only to what routes through the alternate gateway.

Shopify wins on the three things that decide this project: the AI/API ecosystem (the dashboard, the cross-sell selector, and the Klaviyo integration are all built Shopify-first), 12 brands in 8 weeks as a configuration job rather than a dev job, and free native subscriptions — the LTV lever at zero added stack cost.

Decision rule: Shopify, contingent on the kava processor check already underway. If kava is hard-blocked, the fallback is launching Rest & Calm with one product — not switching platforms. Eleven of twelve SKUs have zero payment issues.

Why Klaviyo wins — and where it loses: it loses on price — Omnisend does 80% of the job at 65% of the cost. The premium over Omnisend is ~$24/month; one incremental cross-sector order per month pays it.

Customer Intelligence — OuterSignal

OuterSignal: See Who's Buying (Shopify App Store, Built-for-Shopify badge) turns every order into a full customer profile from publicly available data — demographics, personas, and automatic flagging of celebrities, executives, and influencers in the order stream. Integrates with Klaviyo, Meta, Slack, Recharge, Yotpo, and more. Rating: 5.0 across 17 reviews (100% five-star).

PlanPriceWhat You Get

Pro$199/mo + 3¢ per unique customerAdds historical search across past orders; for stores under 30K orders/mo
  • Two jobs in this build: (1) Market — same-day detection when a customer with reach buys, turning the order stream into an influencer-seeding pipeline; (2) Evaluate — enriched demographics feed Klaviyo segments so sector personas are built from who's actually buying, not who was predicted to.
  • Rollout plan: start Free at launch (100 customers/month covers early volume), upgrade to Starter when volume crosses it — ~$79/month effective at 1,000 customers.
  • Note: the app requests broad data scopes (customer PII, geolocation, order editing) — reflect it in the store's privacy policy.
