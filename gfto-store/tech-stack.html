Executive Brief — Claude as the Gate 0 Operating Interface

Claude connects to every Gate 0 tool through its API — Shopify (orders, products, customers, subscriptions), Klaviyo (email flows, segments, campaigns), Judge.me (reviews), GoAffPro (affiliates), and GA4 (traffic) — using one set of credentials collected at setup. OuterSignal's enrichment is read through Klaviyo profiles.

From that position, Claude runs the store's three functions. Market: every Monday it drafts the week's sector emails and social posts, checks them against the claims guardrail, and schedules approved items directly through the Klaviyo API. Promote: it builds and tunes the automated flows — welcome, abandoned cart, post-purchase, cross-sell. Evaluate: each Sunday night it pulls Shopify, Klaviyo, and GA4 data, computes the per-sector scoreboard, and emails leadership a plain-English Monday summary with one flagged decision.

Day to day, the operator simply asks: "Yesterday's orders?" "Which sector converts best?" One human approval session weekly; Claude operates everything else.

One-time build line (not in the monthly number): the scheduled runtime that lets the AI act on its own — the Sunday-night data pull, Monday summary sender, and credentials glue. Days of build, not weeks; named here so the $95/month figure survives scrutiny.

The two messaging lanes (set at Gate 0, sending starts later): US carriers prohibit business texting about cannabis-family products — no matter how legal they are. So the catalog splits into two reminder channels:

Clean lane (SMS + push): methylene blue products, tryptophan, kava. Restricted lane (web push only): the cannabinoid form factors (infused sugar, tear packs, gum) and — pending carrier review — the hemp-derived pet treats. Web push has no carrier gatekeeper (customers opt in directly on the site), which is why PushOwl enters at Gate 1: it's the retention channel for roughly half the catalog that SMS can never touch. Gate 0's only job for both lanes: collect the consents.

AI Search Visibility (AEO) — Built In From Day One

AI engines — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Claude — don't rank pages; they quote sources they can parse and trust. The store is structured to be quotable. Cost: ~$0 — all configuration and writing, produced by the same content engine.

Why Shopify wins — honestly: it does not win on sticker price (BigCommerce matches it), and it carries one real flag: if kava fails Shopify Payments' acceptable-use review, a third-party high-risk gateway adds a 2% penalty on the Basic plan. Quantified: at $10K/month of kava revenue that's $200/month — annoying, not architecture-changing, and it applies only to what routes through the alternate gateway.

Shopify wins on the three things that decide this project: the AI/API ecosystem (the dashboard, the cross-sell selector, and the Klaviyo integration are all built Shopify-first), 12 brands in 8 weeks as a configuration job rather than a dev job, and free native subscriptions — the LTV lever at zero added stack cost.

Decision rule: Shopify, contingent on the kava processor check already underway. If kava is hard-blocked, the fallback is launching Rest & Calm with one product — not switching platforms. Eleven of twelve SKUs have zero payment issues.

Customer Intelligence — OuterSignal

OuterSignal: See Who's Buying (Shopify App Store, Built-for-Shopify badge) turns every order into a full customer profile from publicly available data — demographics, personas, and automatic flagging of celebrities, executives, and influencers in the order stream. Integrates with Klaviyo, Meta, Slack, Recharge, Yotpo, and more. Rating: 5.0 across 17 reviews (100% five-star).

PlanPriceWhat You Get

Pro$199/mo + 3¢ per unique customerAdds historical search across past orders; for stores under 30K orders/mo
  • Two jobs in this build: (1) Market — same-day detection when a customer with reach buys, turning the order stream into an influencer-seeding pipeline; (2) Evaluate — enriched demographics feed Klaviyo segments so sector personas are built from who's actually buying, not who was predicted to.
  • Rollout plan: start Free at launch (100 customers/month covers early volume), upgrade to Starter when volume crosses it — ~$79/month effective at 1,000 customers.
  • Note: the app requests broad data scopes (customer PII, geolocation, order editing) — reflect it in the store's privacy policy.
