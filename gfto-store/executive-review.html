GF Tech One Executive Review

GFTO Multi-Product D2C Store

The Eight Questions a Leadership Decision Requires
Last Updated: July 20, 2026 Prepared by: Horsepower AI Status: Pre-Launch — Awaiting Gate 0
This is a living document. It reflects the plan as it stands today and will be revised as decisions land, numbers firm up, and circumstances change — treat it as the current state of record, not a fixed proposal frozen in time.
1

What Is Being Proposed

A direct-to-consumer Shopify storefront selling GF Tech One's own formulated product portfolio, organized as a house of brands — each product (Trypt.co, LEUCO Fizz, Purely Elevated Paws, and others) keeps its own identity rather than sitting under one umbrella consumer brand. Launch scope is deliberately narrow: ~6 sellable SKUs already packaged or nearly complete, not the full catalog. Mark Rhodes stands as the public formulator/credibility story across the site. An AI-run marketing engine handles content, distribution, and performance evaluation from day one.

2

Why GFTO Should Do It

This is the direct execution of GFTO's own DTC thesis — find 1,000 customers, product costs $1–3, sell at $10–30, own the infrastructure — applied to formulations Mark has already built that have never been sold at scale. It creates a revenue channel that doesn't depend on any single outside partner, unlike the Easyriders licensing deal, which has stalled for months. It also positions GFTO as a live case study for AI-run direct-to-consumer marketing, at no incremental cost to the company.

3

How It Makes Money

Direct sales across the launch SKUs, structured as a ladder: one-time purchase → subscription (the recurring-revenue engine) → a protected wholesale portal for smoke shops, convenience, and specialty retail → third-party products under revenue-share once the platform is proven. Three built-in guarantees (money-back, verified-dose, feel-it-or-free) lower first-purchase friction without requiring a finished brand story first.

4

What Approval Is Being Requested

  • Green light to move from working brief to active build (Shopify + Gate 0 tool stack live)
  • Real budget dollars for inventory, packaging, and fulfillment — not yet provided, currently the single largest open item
  • Resolution on two compliance-critical questions before any SKU can go live: the per-SKU hemp-derived cannabinoid ruling (0.4mg total THC/container cap, effective Nov 12, 2026) and methylene blue's dietary-supplement status relative to the FDCA drug-exclusion rule
  • Final sign-off on SKU list, pricing, and per-unit COGS from product development — the input the rest of the plan is waiting on
5

How Much It Will Cost

Tooling is revenue-gated: ~$100/month at $0 revenue, rising only as the store earns — roughly $200 → $340 → $545 → $880/month across the $5K / $15K / $40K / $100K monthly-revenue gates, always under ~1.5–3% of revenue. That figure covers software only.

Not yet sized: initial inventory/COGS, packaging, and a fulfillment plan. This is the real-dollar figure leadership still needs to supply.

6

How Long It Will Take

1–2 months from full build to launch-ready, starting from the day final SKU/COGS data lands. The clock does not start until the pre-launch gates close: budget confirmed, legal review cleared, fulfillment process set, and every launch SKU's compliance matrix at 10-for-10. None of those gates are closed as of this writing.

7

What Could Go Wrong

  • Overexpansion — the named top risk: adding SKUs or categories before repeat-purchase behavior and unit economics are proven
  • An adverse legal ruling on either open compliance question could remove SKUs from the launch list entirely
  • Payment-processor restrictions on kava and cannabinoid-adjacent SKUs, a documented issue on standard processors
  • Carrier restrictions — U.S. wireless carriers prohibit SMS marketing for cannabis-adjacent products, closing the primary retention channel for roughly half the catalog (web push is a partial workaround, not a full substitute)
  • Liability exposure if the verified-dose guarantee is marketed before it's backed by real compliance data
